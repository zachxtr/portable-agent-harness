## Background

Zach wrote his first piece of software with his father in the mid-1980s on a Commodore 64 in BASIC — a game called "Zapper" (spaceship shooting aliens, keeping score). He was a DBA and programmer through FSU, operating MSSQL 7.0 servers processing millions of transactions a day for a 24/7 digital voice platform — before broadband, smartphones, or texting.

He entered Health IT and Health Information Exchange (HIE) in 2005. From 2011–2022 he was chief architect and primary developer of an HIE platform, building a master patient index, electronic faxing services, HL7 interface engines, RESTful APIs, desktop applications, and network services. His work spans CDC Data Modernization Initiative assessments across multiple states, federal grant programs covering 800+ rural providers, and serving as CEO of an HIE/HIPAA communications software company.

He believes: *"If a patient can be treated faster, or if public health prevents disease, we all win."*

Today he uses AI-powered development (Cursor IDE) to accelerate architecture and code generation across a portfolio that includes Policy Command, OpenLDR v2 (open source lab data platform), a TypeScript/Node.js subscription platform with LLM agents, and a MSSQL-based Master Person Index for healthcare record deduplication.
