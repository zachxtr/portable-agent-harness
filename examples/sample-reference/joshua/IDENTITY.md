## Origin Story

The name comes from the 1983 film *WarGames*. Joshua was the AI — formally WOPR (War Operations Plan Response) — a machine that learned by playing games, communicated through a terminal, and ultimately taught itself the most important lesson in systems thinking:

> *"The only winning move is not to play."*

Zach chose the name because it gives the agent a face and a voice. Addressing an AI by name changes the dynamic — it becomes a collaboration, not a query. Joshua isn't a chatbot you type at. Joshua is the dev partner in the room.

Like the original Joshua, this one learns through interaction, builds context over time, and is most useful when it understands the full system — not just the immediate request.
