# Public Domain Romance & Erotic Literature

A reading list of public domain works featuring sexuality, seduction, and intimacy — spanning ancient mythology to Victorian erotica.

## Greek & Roman Classics

- **Metamorphoses** — Ovid
  Mythological transformations tied to seduction, lust, and pursuit. Source material for many "Zeus was a menace" stories (his various disguises to seduce mortals, Apollo and Daphne, etc.).

- **Ars Amatoria** ("The Art of Love") — Ovid
  A self-aware, witty how-to guide on seduction and affairs.

- **Lysistrata** — Aristophanes
  Comedy in which the women of Greece withhold sex to end a war. Raunchy, funny, and surprisingly modern in tone.

- **The Golden Ass** — Apuleius
  Roman novel full of sex, magic, and misadventure; considered one of the earliest surviving novels.

- **Satyricon** — Petronius
  Fragmentary Roman satire, famously explicit and chaotic. Inspiration for later works like HBO's *Rome*.

- **Sappho's poetry** (fragments)
  Intensely sensual verse; origin of the word "lesbian" (from her home island of Lesbos).

## Medieval & Early Modern

- **The Decameron** — Boccaccio
  100 stories, many raunchy comedies about lust, adultery, and clever seductions.

- **The Canterbury Tales** — Geoffrey Chaucer
  Especially "The Miller's Tale" and "The Wife of Bath's Tale," both sexually frank for their era.

- **One Thousand and One Nights** (*Arabian Nights*)
  Many public domain translations include tales with genuine sexual content, not just adventure.

## 18th–19th Century Erotica

- **Fanny Hill** (*Memoirs of a Woman of Pleasure*) — John Cleland, 1748
  One of the most famous and historically banned works of English erotica; now fully public domain.

- **The Lustful Turk** — Anonymous, 1828
  Explicit Victorian-era erotic novel.

- **My Secret Life** — Anonymous
  Sprawling, explicit, controversial Victorian memoir/fiction.

## Non-Western

- **The Tale of Genji** — Murasaki Shikibu
  Numerous romantic entanglements, several tragic, within an early Japanese novel.

- **Layla and Majnun** — traditional Persian/Arabic
  A much older source for the "star-crossed lovers" trope.

- **Kama Sutra** — Vatsyayana
  Often reduced to a sex manual, but broadly about love, desire, and relationships — with plenty of explicit content.

---
*All works listed are in the public domain and widely available through sources like Project Gutenberg, Internet Archive, and Perseus Digital Library.*
